About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign . My research focuses on machine learning systems, large language model serving, and energy-efficient AI infrastructure.

I am broadly interested in building efficient, reliable, and scalable systems for real-world AI applications. Recently, I have been working on prefill-decode disaggregation, SLO-aware request scheduling, and multi-agent LLM serving systems.

Previously, I worked on video analytics systems and machine learning for IoT and edge computing. I am especially interested in systems that connect machine learning models with practical deployment constraints such as latency, throughput, energy consumption, and hardware resources.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing violin, learning languages, traveling, and exploring new technologies.
